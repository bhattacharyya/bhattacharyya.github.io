Exploring Data

Reddit Comments
Wed 04 November 2015

This is my first ever data science work that is not directly related to structural bioinformatics. It is a relatively simplistic analysis of the May 2015 reddit public comments dataset posted on Kaggle. I tried to see if comments made at certain times of the day get significantly higher scores. Also, if that pattern depends on the controversiality of the comment.

Even though the analysis was simplistic, I was able to use a combination of tools I described in the previous post to arrive at the answer. Consider this more like a proof of concept. Now that the first hurdle is gone, my next goal is to analyze textual data and derive insight from that.

I am posting my IPython session below. You can also checkout this and other scripts here

----------------------------

This ipython session analyzes the hourly variation in scores of reddit postings over a period of 5 days. It also tries to see if "controversial" postings vary in the scoring pattern than "non-controversial" ones

The two things that stand out from this analysis are :

Reddit entries receive the highest scores between 1 PM to 3 PM GMT &

The average score is more than 10 times higher for the non-controversial comments.
